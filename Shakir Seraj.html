Apollo 11

Apollo 111 was the spacefight that landed the first humans, Americans Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin , on the moon on July 20, 1969, at 20:18 UTC.

       "Armstrong became the first to step onto the lunar surface 6 hours later on July 21 at 02:56 UTC."

Armstrong spent about three and a half two and a half hour outside the spacecraft, Aldrin slightly less; and together they collected 47.5 pounds (21.5kg)
of lunar material for return to Earth.

A third member of the mission, Micheal Collins, piloted the command spacecraft alone in lunar orbit until Armstrong and
Aldrin returned to it for the trip back to Earth.
